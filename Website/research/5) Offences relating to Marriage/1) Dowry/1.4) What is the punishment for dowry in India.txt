

**Punishable Acts under the Dowry Prohibition Act**

The Dowry Prohibition Act specifies the following acts as offences and prescribes punishments for them:

1.  **Demanding Dowry:** Punishable with imprisonment for a term which shall not be less than six months, but which may extend to two years, and with a fine which may extend to ten thousand rupees. (Section 4)

2.  **Taking Dowry:** Punishable with imprisonment for a term which shall not be less than five years, and with a fine which shall not be less than fifteen thousand rupees or the value of the dowry received, whichever is higher. (Section 3)

3.  **Giving Dowry:** The person who gives dowry is also punishable with imprisonment for a term which shall not be less than five years, and with a fine which shall not be less than fifteen thousand rupees or the value of the dowry, whichever is higher. (Section 3)
