
**Powers of the Magistrate**

During the proceedings under the Domestic Violence Act, the Magistrate holds the following powers to ensure a fair and sensitive process:

*   The Magistrate can direct the aggrieved woman and the respondent to undergo counseling at any stage of the proceedings.
*   The Magistrate can seek the assistance of a welfare expert, who should preferably be a woman, to aid the proceedings.
*   The Magistrate may, at any stage, order that the proceedings be conducted in camera (in private) to protect the privacy of the parties involved.
*   The Magistrate is empowered to pass any order based solely on the testimony of the aggrieved woman.
