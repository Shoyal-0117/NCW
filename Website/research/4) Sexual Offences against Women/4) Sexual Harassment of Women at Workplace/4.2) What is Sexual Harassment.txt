
The following unwelcome acts or behavior (whether direct or indirect) are explicitly defined as constituting sexual harassment:

-   Unwanted physical contact and advances.
-   A demand or request for sexual favors.
-   Making remarks with a sexual connotation.
-   Forcibly showing pornography.
-   Any other unwelcome physical, verbal, or non-verbal conduct of a sexual nature.

Furthermore, the Act also specifies that the following circumstances constitute sexual harassment:

-   Any preferential or detrimental treatment in a woman's employment, or a promise or threat of such treatment, linked to her submission to or rejection of unwelcome sexual behavior.
-   Any implied or explicit threat about her present or future employment status.
-   Any conduct that creates an intimidating, offensive, or hostile work environment for her, or which can unreasonably interfere with her work performance.
