

**The Local Committee (LC)**

The Local Committee serves as the external redressal body for specific situations. It is responsible for receiving complaints from:
- Domestic workers.
- Employees of establishments with fewer than 10 workers.
- Workplaces that have not constituted a mandatory Internal Committee (IC).

This committee is formed by the District Officer, who is appointed by the respective State or Central Government. The District Officer must also designate a nodal officer in every block, taluka, or ward to receive complaints from aggrieved women.

**Composition of the Local Committee**

The Local Committee must meet the following requirements:

-   The Chairperson must be a woman who is committed to the cause of women and is well-known in the field of social work.
-   At least two members must be from non-governmental organizations (NGOs) or associations committed to the cause of women. Of these two:
    -   One must have a background in law.
    -   The other must be a woman from the Scheduled Castes (SC), Scheduled Tribes (ST), Other Backward Classes (OBC), or a minority community.
-   One member must be a woman employed in the block, taluka, or ward where the committee is constituted.
