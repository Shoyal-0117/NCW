Urban Local Bodies

Women's participation in urban governance and municipal administration

74th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1992

The 74th Amendment mandated reservation for women in Urban Local Bodies, ensuring their participation in city governance and municipal decision-making.

Types of Urban Local Bodies

•

Municipal Corporations: For large cities with population over 3 lakh, with women's reservation in council seats and mayoral positions.

•

Municipal Councils: For smaller cities and towns, ensuring women's representation in local urban governance.

•

Nagar Panchayats: For transitional areas between rural and urban, with mandatory women's participation.

Reservation Structure

•

33% Seat Reservation: One-third of total seats reserved for women in all urban local bodies.

•

Chairperson Positions: Rotation system ensuring women get opportunity to lead as mayors and chairpersons.

•

Committee Representation: Women's participation in various municipal committees and boards.

Functions and Powers

Urban Local Bodies handle 18 functions listed in the 12th Schedule, including urban planning, water supply, public health, and slum improvement programs.

Key Areas of Impact

•

Urban Planning: Women's perspective in city development, housing, and infrastructure planning.

•

Public Health: Focus on maternal health, child care centers, and sanitation facilities.

•

Safety and Security: Initiatives for women's safety in public spaces and transportation.

•

Social Services: Programs for education, skill development, and women's welfare.

Achievements and Success Stories

•

Women Mayors: Increasing number of women mayors leading major cities across India.

•

Policy Innovation: Introduction of gender-sensitive policies and women-friendly urban infrastructure.

•

Community Engagement: Enhanced citizen participation and community-based urban development.
